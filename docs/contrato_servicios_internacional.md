## PREAMBLE / PREÁMBULO

This Service Agreement (hereinafter "the Agreement") is entered into between the parties identified above, hereinafter referred to as **THE PROVIDER** and **THE CLIENT**, and is governed by internationally recognized legal frameworks including:

- **UNIDROIT Principles of International Commercial Contracts (2016)** — governing general contract formation, performance, and breach.
- **United Nations Convention on Contracts for the International Sale of Goods (CISG, Vienna 1980)** — applied by analogy to service deliverables where applicable.
- **ICC Model International Sale Contract** — as a reference standard for commercial terms.
- **UNCITRAL Model Law on Electronic Commerce (1996)** and **Model Law on Electronic Signatures (2001)** — governing digital communications and signatures.
- **EU General Data Protection Regulation (GDPR, Regulation 2016/679)** — applicable when personal data of EU residents is processed.
- **OECD Guidelines on the Protection of Privacy and Transborder Flows of Personal Data** — as a global reference for data handling.
- **World Intellectual Property Organization (WIPO) Copyright Treaty (1996)** — governing ownership of software and digital works.
- **Berne Convention for the Protection of Literary and Artistic Works** — as adopted by the parties' respective countries.

Both parties declare they have full legal capacity to enter into this Agreement, which is binding upon signature or electronic acceptance.

## CLAUSE 4: SCOPE, CHANGES & VERSION CONTROL

### 4.1 Scope Freeze

The Project scope is defined in Annex A. Once this Agreement is signed, any modification to the scope requires the issuance of a **Change Order** signed by both parties, detailing: description of the change, cost impact, and schedule impact.

### 4.2 Change Order Procedure

All change requests shall follow this workflow:

1. THE CLIENT submits the request in writing to the Project Manager.
2. THE PROVIDER issues a cost and schedule impact assessment within three (3) business days.
3. THE CLIENT approves or rejects within three (3) business days.
4. If approved, the Change Order is signed and integrated into the project schedule.

THE PROVIDER is not obligated to begin any change work until the Change Order is duly signed and the corresponding payment confirmed.

### 4.3 Revision Rounds

Each deliverable includes up to **two (2) rounds of revisions** at no additional cost. From the third revision onward, an hourly rate of USD/EUR _______ (or as specified in Annex A) will apply, billed in one (1) hour increments.

## CLAUSE 9: CONFIDENTIALITY & DATA PROTECTION

Both parties agree to maintain strict confidentiality over all commercial, technical, and strategic information exchanged during the term of this Agreement and for a period of **three (3) years** after its termination, in accordance with UNIDROIT Principles Art. 2.1.16.

### 9.1 Personal Data (GDPR & International Standards)

Where the Project involves processing personal data of individuals located in the European Union or European Economic Area, the parties shall comply with **Regulation (EU) 2016/679 (GDPR)**. Where applicable:

- THE PROVIDER acts as a **Data Processor** and THE CLIENT acts as a **Data Controller**.
- THE PROVIDER shall process personal data only on documented instructions from THE CLIENT.
- THE PROVIDER shall implement appropriate technical and organizational security measures (Art. 32 GDPR).
- THE PROVIDER shall not engage sub-processors without THE CLIENT's prior written consent.
- Upon termination, THE PROVIDER shall delete or return all personal data as instructed by THE CLIENT.

For data transfers across borders, the parties shall rely on Standard Contractual Clauses (SCCs) or other lawful transfer mechanisms recognized under applicable data protection law.

### 9.2 General Data Security

Regardless of GDPR applicability, THE PROVIDER commits to applying industry-standard security practices (encryption in transit, access controls, secure credential management) to all data provided by THE CLIENT.

## CLAUSE 11: TERMINATION

Either party may terminate this Agreement in the following circumstances:

- Material breach of the obligations set forth herein, upon **fifteen (15) business days'** prior written notice and failure to cure within such period, consistent with UNIDROIT Principles Art. 7.3.1.
- Insolvency, bankruptcy, or cessation of business of either party.
- Mutual written agreement between the parties.

In the event of termination attributable to THE CLIENT, THE PROVIDER shall retain payments received proportional to work completed and may invoice for any additional work in progress at the time of termination. In the event of termination attributable to THE PROVIDER, payments corresponding to unperformed work shall be refunded, net of documented expenses already incurred.

---

## CLAUSE 12: FORCE MAJEURE

Neither party shall be liable for failure to perform its obligations resulting from events beyond its reasonable control, including but not limited to: natural disasters, acts of government authority, wars, pandemics, widespread internet or power infrastructure failures, or cyberattacks by third parties, consistent with UNIDROIT Principles Art. 7.1.7.

The affected party must notify the other within **three (3) business days** of the event's occurrence. If the force majeure event persists for more than **sixty (60) calendar days**, either party may terminate the Agreement by written notice without further liability, except for payment of work already completed.
